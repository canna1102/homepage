Last-mile Delivery Visualization

Orientation

Contest

Abstract

Last-mile describes the delivery of packages from a transportation hub to a final destination in logistics. Last-mile scenario is often complicated because it contains time, people and route dimensions, making the understanding of how a busy logistic system works difficult.

In this contest, we are provided with package delivering data in one day in the city of Shanghai. We simulate thousands of couriers carrying packages as moving particles on the map. With this visual design, package delivery patterns, such as O2O order peak at noon and intensive overtime deliveries under abnormal conditions can be quickly caught.

Besides, we use some visual analytical designs to make the data visualization more thought-invoking. For example, hub on map is mapped as circled marker, with radius reflecting the number of packages comes from it. Larger marker means the hub is taking heavier tasks than others. When user mousing over a marker, the destinations of the hub, together with a route connecting them will be shown. This design allows users to find how dense is the package destinations located in the area of a hub. We also use the logistics data for courier profiling. That is, we generate a score for each courier based on the punctuality and delivery efficiency. It is then interesting to find couriers of high and low scores and compare their delivery patterns.

Technique

D3.js, Mapbox-gl.js
