Last-mile Delivery Visualization

Orientation

Contest

Abstract

In logistics, last－mile describes the delivery of packages from a transportation hub to a final destination. Efficient plan of last-mile delivery is a challenging research problem involving global optimization of time, people and route.

In this contest, we provide visualization as tools to show deliver efficiency of different planning results. Based on the plan data, we model couriers carrying packages as moving particles on map. As overtime is a severe issue, particles are colored red to highlight these cases. With this design, analysts can quickly answer questions such as if the logistic system runs properly and where do the overtime cases happen.

The visualization system is further extended to data analytics. For example, we provide visual cues to indicate if a transportation hub is located reasonably. To this aim, we map each hub on map as a circled marker, with radius reflecting the number of packages comes from it. Larger marker means the hub is taking heavier tasks than others. When user mousing over a marker, the destinations of the hub, together with a route connecting them will be shown. This design allows users to see how dense is the package destinations located in the area of a hub. The logistics data is also used for courier profiling. That is, we generate a score for each courier based on the punctuality and delivery efficiency. It is then interesting to find couriers of high and low scores and compare their delivery patterns.

Technique

D3.js, Mapbox-gl.js
