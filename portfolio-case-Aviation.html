Abstract

Spatio-temporal variability has been an interesting research problem in many fields. In this project, we analyze the transportation data in Shenzhen with spatio-temporal methods. First, we match geo locations with positions along road paths by road-matching methods. After that, the vehicles run on each road at each time frame can be identified. We then calculate the traffic status (real time average / median speed of vehicles on the road map), and visualize the information on the map. Next, we want to provide users with a tool to inspect how the traffic is formed. To this aim, the visual analytics system is designed as shown in the image above. The left map shows traffic in a specific region set by the user. The right map plays a fast video of the trajectory of individual vehicles. Now users can easily explore the traffic with spatio-temporal filter and inspect the actual vehicle movements behind traffic information.
