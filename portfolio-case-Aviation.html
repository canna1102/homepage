Gate assignment simulation

Contest

Abstract

This is an invited work for the Big Data in Aviation contest hosted by Tianchi, Alibaba.

The data visualization demo shows how airplanes take use of parking resources in the Baiyun airport, Guangzhou. We convert the records of gate assignment schedule into temporal-spatio usage patterns of gates for visual analytics. We also simulate how schedule works by animating airplanes running on runways, departing and arriving at the airport.

The airport model is taken from the real CAD blueprint. We extract and reconstruct the terminal parts in the visualization system. Furthermore, motion orbits for airplanes of each gate are imitated as uniform-speed Tween animation over runways. To increase the usability of data exploration, the system allows users interacting with airplane glyphs. For example, when clicking a rule-broke flight that is colored by the warning yellow, users and find the detailed information causing the broken in the tooltip opening up.

The whole system is designed as a large screen data visualization for real-time gate assignment data. The screen recording of it is presented at the Yunqi conference by Alibaba.
