Gate assignment simulation

Orientation

Contest

Abstract

This project is invited by Tianchi Big Data Aviation innovative contest.

In this contest, the contestants applied various optimization approaches to maximize gate usage rate. However, in terms of improvement evaluation, the objective indicators, i.e. usage of near gate, often fail to give the audience an concrete picture of what has changed. Our visualization system, appearing as a large screen application, is born to simulate the optimized gate assignment results and allow audience to see how approaches work differently.

To this aim, we first model the gates and main runways of Baiyun airport from a CAD blueprint. Airplane motion orbits for each gate is further constructed to simulate airplanes departing and arriving at an apron. The output of gate assignment approach becomes an animated show after airport modeling. In additional to the objective indicators, we also visualize gate usage efficiency at real-time, by providing a shifting area graph in the system. Users can interact with each flight, for example, a rule-broke flight that is colored by the warning yellow, to open up tooltip for more information.

In addition, some visual analysis is performed to reveal the patterns in gate assignment. For example, we use a heat map visualization to show how frequently each gate is put into usage.
