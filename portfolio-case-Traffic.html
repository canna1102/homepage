Research Project

Abstract

Spatio-temporal variability has been an interesting research problem in many fields. In this project, we analyze spatio-temporal traffic patterns with the transportation data in Shenzhen.

We take three steps to process the data. First, the geo locations collected from vehicles are bound to roads. This road-matching step makes it possible to identify which vehicle is running on which road at one time frame. After that, we calculate 'traffic' as the average / median speed of vehicles on road. At last, taking all roads and all time frames into consideration, we get a speed map reflecting the traffic conditions in real-time.

Next, we want to provide users with a tool to inspect how the traffic is formed. To this aim, the visual analytics system is designed as shown in the image above. The left map shows traffic in a specific region set by the user. The right map plays a fast video of the trajectory of individual vehicles. Now users can easily explore the traffic with spatio-temporal filter and inspect the actual vehicle movements behind traffic information.
